Welcome to The Vault 👋

The Vault is your personal AI assistant. Think of it as having a brilliant, tireless helper who remembers everything, never judges, and is always available whenever you need them.

Get started in 5 minutes → Browse all features →

Getting started

Your first 5 minutes

Getting up and running only takes three steps:

1
Start the dashboard

Open a terminal and run python vault_dashboard.py. This starts The Vault. You’ll see a menu you can navigate with your keyboard. If this is your first time, a setup guide will appear.

2
Ask your first question

Type anything you want help with. Try “What can you help me with today?” or “Set up my health tracking.” The Vault will respond in plain English.

3
Tell it about yourself

The Vault works best when it knows a bit about you. Try: “My daily water target is 2 litres”, “I take metformin in the mornings”, or “My fitness goal is 10,000 steps a day.” It will remember.

✓
You can’t break anything
The Vault will ask for your approval before doing anything that can’t be undone — like sending an email. You’re always in control.
Communication

How to talk to The Vault

The Vault understands plain, everyday English. No special commands. No technical words. Just describe what you want, the same way you’d ask a friend.

Examples

📅 Scheduling something
You: “Add a dentist appointment on Thursday at 2pm.”
Vault: “Done — dentist added for Thursday 2:00 PM. Want a reminder the day before?”
💗 Tracking health
You: “I slept for 7 hours last night. Quality was about a 7 out of 10.”
Vault: “Logged! That’s your third night in a row above 7 hours — you’re doing great.”
🛒 Shopping help
You: “I need to do a grocery shop. Making pasta this week and I’m low on bread.”
Vault: “Here’s your list: pasta sauce, spaghetti, parmesan, bread. Want me to check the ALDI specials first?”
🎯 Setting a goal
You: “I want to drink more water. Set my goal to 2.5 litres a day.”
Vault: “Hydration target updated to 2,500 ml per day. Want a midday reminder if you haven’t hit 1,000 ml?”
💡
Be as specific or as vague as you like
If you say “I went to the gym,” The Vault will ask for more details if it needs them. You don’t have to get it perfect the first time.
⚠
Some things need your approval first
Sending emails, posting to social media, or making financial records are “gated” — The Vault always asks before anything like that happens.

Help

🔒 Approvals & safety

The Vault has a safety system that prevents it from doing anything irreversible without your explicit permission.

✓
What The Vault can do without asking

Log health data, remember things, add calendar events, update shopping lists, track expenses, draft emails (not send), draft social posts (not publish).

🔒
What always needs your “yes” first

Sending email, posting to social media, making real-world financial records, deleting data, connecting to any external service.

💡
If The Vault asks “Are you sure?” — that’s a good thing
It means it’s about to do something that affects the outside world. Read the confirmation and only approve if it looks right.
Help

🔒 Your privacy

💻
Stored only on your computer

All health data, journal entries, financial records, and personal notes are stored as files on your own machine. They never leave unless you explicitly export them.

🧠
What goes to AI services

When The Vault needs an AI model to process a request, only the specific text for that request is sent. Your health profile, journal, and financial data are never included in AI prompts.

🔑
API keys and credentials

Your API keys are stored in a local .env file on your computer. They are never committed to GitHub or shared anywhere.

Help

❓ Frequently asked questions

Does The Vault need the internet to work?

Most features — schedule, health, goals, wardrobe, shopping — work completely offline. Features that need internet: email, weather, web research, and AI image generation.

Can The Vault read my private journal?

No. Journal entries are stored locally and never passed to any AI by default. The Vault only reads your journal if you explicitly ask it to.

What if The Vault makes a mistake?

Just say so: “That’s wrong — I actually slept 8 hours, not 6.” It will update the record. All data is stored in plain files you can edit directly if needed.

Can The Vault post to my social media?

No — it can only draft posts. You review and publish yourself. The Vault never posts on your behalf.

How do I back up my data?

All your data is in the The_Vault folder on your computer. Back it up like any important folder — copy to an external drive or cloud. Or say “Run a backup” for an automated timestamped archive.

Can I use The Vault on my phone?

Currently The Vault runs on your computer only. A mobile interface is on the roadmap.

What’s the difference between tasks and goals?

A task is something to do today or this week — a single action. A goal is a bigger outcome you’re working toward over weeks or months, broken into smaller tasks.

Does The Vault connect to Google Calendar or Apple Calendar?

Not automatically yet. The Vault has its own internal schedule. Calendar export/import integration is on the roadmap.

📖 Glossary — plain English

API key
A password-like code that lets The Vault talk to an external service. Stored privately on your computer.
Approval gate
A safety checkpoint. The Vault stops and asks “Are you sure?” before doing anything that affects the outside world.
Dashboard
The main screen when you start The Vault. Shows your daily overview and gives you a menu.
Dry run
A practice run — The Vault shows you what it would do without actually doing it.
Feature page
A detailed guide for a specific capability, like the period tracker or shopping assistant.
Human Input
A task that needs a human to complete it — like entering a password or making a phone call.
Living schedule
The Vault’s smart calendar that reorganises tasks around your availability automatically.
Mission
A larger project The Vault is actively working on — something that takes multiple steps over multiple sessions.
Persona
One of the 20 specialist AI assistants inside The Vault. Each has a specific job.
Prompt
What you type to The Vault. Any message you send is a prompt.
Routine
A repeating step-by-step checklist that runs on a schedule, like a morning routine.
VOD
A recording of a past stream. The Vault can track and organise your VOD library.
.env file
A hidden config file on your computer that stores API keys privately. Never shared or uploaded.
