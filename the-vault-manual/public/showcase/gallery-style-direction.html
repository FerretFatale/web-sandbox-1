Public Showcase · Style Direction

Style direction that feels premium without losing readability

The showcase should feel warm, polished, and confident. It should never drift into system-font scraps or a washed-out planning page. This direction keeps the public area visually cohesive while preserving the darker portal authority.

Visual personality

Background

Deep amber-black base to keep the page grounded and readable.

Surface

Warm panels with enough contrast to separate sections clearly.

Accent

Gold accent for links, labels, and proof highlights.

Copy style guardrails

  • Show the work, do not oversell it.
  • Keep the tone confident, not salesy.
  • Use plain labels and concrete evidence references.
  • Never bury navigation inside body copy when the topnav can carry it.

Accessibility baseline

  • Readable line length and generous spacing.
  • Consistent heading hierarchy.
  • Responsive cards and obvious link affordance.
  • Dark-theme contrast preserved for public readability.
